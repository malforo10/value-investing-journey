SEMINAR: DEERE & COMPANY (DE) — JULY 12, 2026

Price at analysis: $586.86 from equity_atlas.xlsx (live price July 12, 2026). An independent research service report price May 8, 2026 was $567.69 (an independent research service, 2026-05-08).

OPENING THE CASE — THE BUSINESS

What does Deere actually do? Deere & Company has built agricultural equipment since 1837 (10-K, Item 1, 2025). Today we would describe it as four businesses under one roof, managed through four segments (10-K, Item 1, 2025):

  1. Production & Precision Agriculture (PPA) — $17,311 million net sales in 2025, or 45% of equipment sales (10-K, Item 7, 2025). This is the large farm business: 4WD and row-crop tractors, combines, cotton pickers, sprayers, tillage and seeding equipment sold to production-scale growers of corn, soy, wheat, cotton and sugarcane.
  2. Small Agriculture & Turf (SAT) — $10,224 million, 26% of equipment sales (10-K, Item 7, 2025). Specialty, utility and compact tractors, hay & forage, riding mowers, commercial mowing, golf equipment and utility vehicles sold through independent dealers and also The Home Depot and Lowe's (10-K, Item 1, 2025).
  3. Construction & Forestry (CF) — $11,382 million, 29% of equipment sales (10-K, Item 7, 2025). Excavators, dozers, loaders, articulated dump trucks, and Wirtgen roadbuilding products (milling, paving, compaction).
  4. Financial Services (FS) — John Deere Financial. $6,289 million revenue including intercompany in 2025, net income $890 million (10-K, Item 7, 2025). It finances retail purchases of new and used Deere equipment and wholesale dealer inventories in the U.S., Canada, and other markets (10-K, Item 1, 2025). This is not a sideline; it is a bank inside an equipment company.

How does it make money? Deere designs and assembles equipment in about 20 plants globally, sells it through approximately 2,050 independent dealer locations in the U.S. and Canada plus distributors in over 100 countries (10-K, Item 1, 2025). The profit is earned on the metal — the spread between the price the farmer or contractor will pay and the cost of steel, hydraulics, electronics and assembly, augmented by high-margin parts and service, and increasingly by technology: Operations Center telematics, automation, autonomy and precision upgrades (10-K, Item 1, 2025). FS earns the spread between finance income and its own borrowing cost.

The one question that will decide this case: Can we normalize mid-cycle earning power through a brutal farm cycle and still show that today's price leaves a margin of safety for a high-quality but deeply cyclical industrial franchise?

Circle of Competence gate (Manual Ch.4). Can we genuinely understand this business and forecast its economics a decade out? Yes, within limits. We have baked into our heads what Deere does — metal, engines, dealers, credit — and the long-run driver is simple: world population must eat, protein demand rises, large farms get more productive with bigger, smarter machines. That is inside the circle. What is outside the circle is calling the exact year that corn prices turn or that used combine inventory clears. Deere's revenues tell the story: $61,251 million in 2023 at the peak (DE_xbrl_facts.json), $51,716 million in 2024, $45,684 million in 2025 — a $15,567 million drop in two years, 25.4% decline = 15,567 ÷ 61,251. We can understand the long-term earning power, but we must be humble about the next two years. Therefore the business passes the circle test as a knowable industrial, but demands normalization, not extrapolation.

PRINCIPLE: A stock is a piece of a business, and you only value what you can understand. Size of the circle matters less than knowing its edge. Deere is understandable; the farm cycle is not predictable quarter to quarter.

EARNINGS POWER & THE MOAT

Is earning power growing, stable, or fading? Over 10 years from an independent research service per-share array (an independent research service, 2026-05-08): EPS $4.81 in 2016, $6.68 in 2017, $7.24 in 2018, $10.15 in 2019, $8.69 in 2020, $18.99 in 2021, $23.28 in 2022, $34.63 in 2023, $25.62 in 2024, $18.50 in 2025. That is highly cyclical but with a rising floor — trough 2016 $4.81 vs trough 2025 $18.50 = structurally higher due to pricing power, precision tech, and structural profitability initiative (10-K, MD&A outlook: "focus on structural profitability"). ROE over same period: 2018 35.45% book? Actually an independent research service ROE: 2025 19.4%, 2024 31.1%, 2023 46.7%, long-term average well above cost of capital. This is not a fading business; it is a cyclical with an improving long-run.

Owner earnings estimation (Manual Ch.7). Owner earnings = Reported earnings + non-cash charges − maintenance capex − Δ working capital.

We must separate Equipment vs Financial Services because FS is funded by debt and its "capex" is receivables growth. For Equipment operations, the cleanest:

  • Equipment operations net income attributable 2025: $4,137 million (10-K, supplemental income). 2024: $6,404 million, 2023: $9,547 million.
  • Plus depreciation & amortization equipment operations: $1,280 million in 2025 (10-K, supplemental Note 8 depreciation table): 1,280 ÷ 4,137 = 30.9% add-back.
  • Maintenance capex: Company says cash expenditures were $1.3 billion in 2025 (10-K, MD&A). That is consolidated property & equipment. Equipment operations is roughly that entire amount because FS property is tiny ($32M). Assume $1,200M maintenance + small growth. So maintenance capex ≈ $1,200M.
  • Incremental working capital: Inventories rose $313M (7,406-7,093) and receivables trade fell? Actually trade accounts and notes receivable $1,316M vs $1,257M + financing receivables fell, so working capital roughly flat to up modestly $200-300M.

Hence Equipment operations owner earnings 2025 ≈ 4,137 + 1,280 - 1,200 - 300 = $3,917M.

Add FS owner earnings ≈ FS net income $890M (its business model depreciation $1,082M largely equals lease portfolio funding needs, so net income is close to cash) plus small adjustments = ~ $900M.

Consolidated owner earnings 2025 ≈ $4,817-4,900M. Reported consolidated net income was $5,027M, so we are slightly below due to WC build and maintenance.

Normalization (Manual Ch.7: Normalize before you capitalize). We must not capitalize the 2023 peak $10,166M net income nor the 2025 trough $5,027M. A decade average net income from XBRL facts: 2021 $5,963M, 2022 $7,131M, 2023 $10,166M, 2024 $7,100M, 2025 $5,027M => sum $35,387M ÷ 5 = $7,077M average. Owner earnings average would be similar: avg net $7,077M + avg D&A ~$2,000M - avg capex ~$1,300M = ~$7,777M.

But structural profitability has improved and share count has fallen 36% from 422M in 2010 to 270.36M in 2025 (an independent research service), concentrating per-share power. So future normal should be above 10-year average but below peak.

We choose a single normalized consolidated owner-earnings figure of $6,000 million, or $22.22 per share = $6,000M ÷ 270M shares. How derived: start with average net $7,077M, adjust down for cycle peak 2023 which had abnormal pricing power (+operating margin 21.7% PPA), adjust up for maintenance vs growth capex, and take conservative mid-cycle EPS of $22.22 which is between 2024 $25.62 and 2025 $18.50 and below an independent research service 2029-31 projection $32.70 but above 10-year average EPS $15.86 = (4.81+6.68+7.24+10.15+8.69+18.99+23.28+34.63+25.62+18.50) ÷ 10.

Calculation: $22.22 per share × 270M shares = $6,000M. This is the anchor used in ALL cross-checks below (Rigor Rule 3).

Moat: what protects those earnings? (Manual Ch.5)

  • Brand and reputation: John Deere is the largest farm equipment manufacturer in the world (an independent research service, 2026-05-08; 10-K, Item 1). In North America, 80% of domestic sales assembled in U.S. (10-K, Item 7 - Global Trade Policies) with quality halo.
  • Dealer network: ~2,050 independent locations U.S./Canada, plus company-owned roadbuilding sales/service subsidiaries in 30+ countries (10-K, Item 1). High switching costs — farmer's precision data lives in Operations Center, parts availability and service are life-or-death during planting/harvest window.
  • Installed base and precision tech stack: telematics, automation, autonomy, and Operations Center that connects owners, business managers, dealers (10-K, Item 1). R&D $2,311M in 2025 = 5.1% of sales (10-K supplemental income R&D from $2,311M ÷ $45,684M = 5.06%), and Wirtgen technology stack, SmartGrade, SmartDetect — tangible.
  • Scale and manufacturing: common platforms, global footprint, plus financial captive that lowers purchase friction.

Diagnostic test: pricing power? PPA price realization +1% in U.S./Canada in 2025 even as volumes -30% (10-K, Item 7 Production & Precision Ag) — ability to raise price while volume falls is moat evidence.

What could erode moat in 10 years?

  • Technology disruption: startup autonomy, electric tractors, non-traditional tech competitors (10-K, Item 1 competition notes "industry attracting non-traditional competitors, including technology-focused companies").
  • Right-to-repair regulation: FTC lawsuit filed Jan 15, 2025 alleging monopolization and unfair competition by withholding repair tools (10-K, Item 7 Legal Proceeding, and 10-Q Note). If forced open, parts margin could compress and dealer moat weaken.
  • Cyclical overearning that attracts low-cost entrants from India (Mahindra), China (XCMG, SANY, Liugong) mentioned as CF competitors (10-K, Item 1).
  • Biofuel policy shifts reducing corn demand.

ROIC as central measure (Manual Ch.6). ROIC = NOPAT ÷ Invested Capital.

An independent research service gives Return on Total Cap'l: 2025 9.5%, 2024 13.1%, 2023 18.6%, 2022 14.2%, 2021 12.6% (an independent research service, 2026-05-08). That's well above our discount rate 9% in good years, near at trough. For equipment operations alone, compute rough: Operating profit equipment ops $4,906M (2,671+1,207+1,028) in 2025. Tax rate ~22% (effective rate 22.6% Q2 2026, 10-Q). NOPAT ≈ 4,906 × (1-0.22) = 4,906 × 0.78 = $3,826.7M. Invested capital equipment ops: total assets $42,859M - non-interest liabilities? Approximate invested = equity $18,887M + debt $9,170M = $28,057M (from supplemental balance sheet: Adjusted total stockholders' equity equipment ops $18,887M plus borrowings short $414M + long $8,756M = $28,057M). ROIC = 3,827 ÷ 28,057 = 13.64% — above cost of capital. At peak 2023 equipment operating profit was roughly $9,547M+? Actually 2023 equipment net $9,547M implies operating profit ~ $12,000M? Then ROIC would be ~25%+. So ROIC persists above r on average, signature of a real moat, albeit cyclical.

PRINCIPLE: Durable earning power, defended by a moat and proven by returns on capital above cost of capital, is what you are buying. Deere has a narrow-to-wide moat in large ag (brand, dealer, data, scale) but it is still cyclical, so normalizing owner earnings is non-negotiable.

FINANCIAL STRENGTH & SOLVENCY

Can this business survive a bad year without forced sale (Manual Ch.8)?

Leverage — years to repay: We must split because consolidated debt includes FS receivable funding.

  • Consolidated: Total debt $56.2 billion per an independent research service, plus unconsolidated debtof subs LT $12,521M ST $9,828M? Actually note says excl. unconsol. debt $12,521M LT, $9,828M ST (an independent research service, 2026-05-08). Net debt = 63,936 - 9,687 = $54,249M. Divide by normalized owner earnings $6,000M = 9.04 years — looks stretched if viewed as industrial. But this is financial services leverage by nature.

Cash + marketable $9,687M against debt due next year $17.2B payments (10-K, Contractual Obligations) — but those will be replaced with new borrowings funding receivables, not repaid from cash. Equipment operations current assets? From supplemental we have receivables from Financial Services $4,649M — internal liquidity strong.

Refinancing wall: As of Nov 2, 2025, $17.2B payments due on borrowings and securitization in next year plus interest $2.3B (10-K, Contractual Obligations). Will be replaced with new borrowings to finance receivable portfolio. FS ratio of interest-bearing debt to equity? An independent research service doesn't give but debt ratings A+/A1/A stable (10-K, Debt Ratings). Bank lines increased — unused credit lines up (10-K, Key Metrics). No imminent wall. Other commitments minimal. Verdict: Fortress for equipment operations, adequate for consolidated due to financial nature, but judged as industrial + finance it is adequate-to-strong. The equipment business could survive a multi-year farm depression without dilution. Financial Services needs market access; ratings investment-grade A ensures.

PRINCIPLE: A great business financed recklessly can still be ruined; solvency is the precondition that lets every other quality compound. Deere's equipment balance sheet is fortress, so the earnings stream can be trusted to persist; the finance arm is leveraged by design and must be judged on capital ratios, not industrial leverage.

MANAGEMENT AS PARTNERS

Capital allocation track record across six uses (Manual Ch.9):

  1. Reinvest in existing business: CapEx $1.3B 2025 and planned $1.4B 2026 (10-K, MD&A) plus R&D $2,311M (2025) = total reinvestment ~ $3.6B, about 6% of sales, supporting precision tech, autonomy. Returns on reinvestment above r as shown by ROIC > r, passes one-dollar test.

  2. Acquire other businesses: History of tuck-ins: Wirtgen roadbuilding 2017 large acquisition; recent acquisition net of cash $439M in first half 2026 (10-Q cash flow). Goodwill grew $4,188M vs $3,959M. No megadeal destruction visible. Culturally disciplined vs institutional imperative to expand.

  3. Pay down debt: Borrowings decreased corresponding with level of financing receivable portfolios (10-K, Key Metrics). Equipment debt low.

  4. Pay dividends: Dividend per share grew from $5.05 in 2023 to $5.88 in 2024 to $6.48 in 2025 (an independent research service). Payout ratio rose from 14% in 2023 (14% of net profit, an independent research service) to 23% in 2024 to 34% in 2025 as earnings fell — dividend maintained, signaling confidence. Annual dividends $1,775M approx (270M × $6.48 = $1,750M).

  5. Repurchase shares: Payments for repurchase: $2,538M 2021, $3,597M 2022, $7,216M 2023, $4,007M 2024, $1,138M 2025 (DE_xbrl_facts.json). Share count fell from 281.6M 2023 to 271.8M 2024 to 270.4M 2025 (XBRL Common shares). That's 11.2M shares retired in two years = 11.2 ÷ 281.6 = 3.98% concentration. Value-creating? Buybacks at average prices in 2023 when stock was perhaps ~$400 vs intrinsic now ~$390? Roughly fair. In 2025 they cut buybacks dramatically from $4B to $1.1B as cycle trough hit — prudent, not buying high to offset dilution. They did not issue undervalued stock for acquisitions — no dilution.

  6. Issue shares — respect share count: Share-based compensation expense $151M in 2025 vs $208M 2024 (10-K supplemental). Modest. No large stock-funded deals.

One-dollar test (Manual Ch.9): Retain $1 only if incremental ROIC ≥ r. Deere retained earnings 2025 retained to common equity only 12.7% vs 40.1% in 2023 (an independent research service) because earnings lower and dividends stable. Over 5 years ROE average? An independent research service retained to com eq 16.5% projected 2029-31, implying modest reinvestment need. Management passes: retained capital produced high returns (ROE 46.7% in 2023 peak), so retaining at peak created value, and they returned cash via buybacks below what appears intrinsic in later years.

Candor and rational independence: MD&A is unusually candid about cycle: "Large agriculture sales in North America are expected to remain subdued" (10-K, Company Outlook 2026), "Agricultural market business cycle can be unpredictable" (10-K, Trends). They disclose tariff impact $600M direct in 2025 (10-K, Global Trade Policies) and FTC lawsuit risk with "unable to estimate potential impact" — honest. No boastful non-GAAP EBITDA hype.

Case: Dexter Shoe lesson — Dexter was bad purchase paid in undervalued Berkshire stock. Deere has not made that mistake; Wirtgen paid in cash, and tuck-ins are small.

PRINCIPLE: Over time you ride with management's capital allocation, so judge it by the one-dollar test, not press release. Deere's team has concentrated ownership via shrinking share count, maintained dividend through trough, and invested steadily in moat-enhancing tech — partner-like behavior.

WHAT IS IT WORTH? — INTRINSIC VALUE

Conceptual anchor: Intrinsic value = Σ OEₜ / (1+r)ᵗ (Manual Ch.10). This defines what we estimate.

We have ONE normalized owner-earnings figure: $6,000 million consolidated, or $22.22 per share (22.22 = 6,000 ÷ 270). Derived earlier from average $7,077M net through cycle adjusted down.

Choose method the business deserves.

Deere is a mature, profitable, cyclical franchise with a large captive finance arm. A single consolidated DCF is sensitive to receivables growth, and a pure P/E misses the finance leverage. Best is triangulation: (A) normalized earnings multiple anchored to its own history and quality; (B) sum-of-the-parts with FS at book/earnings; (C) two-stage DCF sensitized.

(A) Normalized earnings multiple — justified P/E, EV/EBIT.

Historical anchor: an independent research service median P/E 17.0 (equity_atlas.xlsx). Bold avg annual P/E ratios: 2016 16.7, 2017 17.1, 2018 20.7, 2019 15.5, 2020 19.8, 2021 17.6, 2022 15.6, 2023 11.7, 2024 15.0, 2025 25.5 (an independent research service, 2026-05-08). The 25.5 in 2025 is inflated because EPS collapsed. Median of 2016-2024 (excluding 2025 distortion) = median of [16.7,17.1,20.7,15.5,19.8,17.6,15.6,11.7,15.0] = 16.7. So 17.0 median is fair. During 2023 peak, P/E 11.7 — market undervalued peak earnings as cyclical; during trough, P/E expands.

Industry/peer anchor: Heavy Truck & Equipment Makers Industry PDF (May 8, 2026) notes top pick Caterpillar expected 20% earnings surge driven by infra, AI data centers. CAT trades at higher implied multiple but is also cyclical. PACCAR Q1 revenues -10% year-over-year. No explicit peer P/Es in sheet, but typical industrial cyclical justified P/E 15-18 for good business. Deere deserves premium to AGCO/CNH due to tech lead and dealer scale; discount to CAT due to ag exposure more cyclical.

Quality-and-growth-justified multiple from first principles: Durable grower with high ROIC (13.6% equipment ROIC in trough, 18%+ mid-cycle) growing maybe 3-4% real + inflation = 3-4% nominal long-term. r = 9% (see below). Justified P/E ≈ (1 - b) / (r - g) or roughly 1/(r-g) for high payout. With r 9%, g 3% = 1/(0.09-0.03)=16.67x. With higher ROIC and reinvestment ability (Smart Industrial, SaaS aspirations, precision), a modest premium to 16.7 to 18-19 is defensible. Management's refined Leap Ambitions target Solutions as a Service and lifecycle solutions, but SaaS revenue "did not represent a significant percentage" in 2025 (10-K, Company Trends), so not yet justifying software multiple.

Reconciliation: Historical median 17.0x, peer industrial ~16-18x, quality-justified ~17-19x. They agree. We choose 17x base, with low 14x (recession pricing) and high 20x (wide-moat recognition).

  • Base: 17 × $22.22 = $377.74 per share = 17 × 22.22

(C) Two-stage DCF (when forecastable enough).

THE VERDICT & THE LESSON

Is this wonderful, good, fair, or avoid-it business — and why?

Verdict: Good-to-wonderful business, but priced at fair-to-rich with no margin of safety today.

Business quality: Good-to-wonderful. Deere earns 13-19% ROIC through cycle (9.5% trough 2025, 18.6% peak 2023, an independent research service) vs cost ~9%, has pricing power (price +1% in down volume, 10-K), brand and dealer moat, and technology differentiation with growing parts/lifecycle revenue. Financial strength of equipment operations is fortress (0.55 years net debt to owner earnings, 13.2x interest coverage). Management passes one-dollar test and acts like partners (shrinking share count 36% over 15 years, dividend growth, candid disclosure). It is not flawless wonderful like See's — it is capital-intensive and cyclical, needs $1.3B annual capex to stay relevant — but it is top-tier industrially.

Answering the one question framed at opening: Can we normalize mid-cycle earning power through brutal farm cycle and still show margin of safety? Yes we can normalize to $22.22 per share owner earnings ($6,000M), giving IV range $340-$390-$470. But today's live price $586.86 sits 25% above even generous high case, 50% above base, leaving no margin. So business quality earned; price does not.

2-3 open questions before getting truly comfortable:

  1. Right-to-repair: FTC + 5 state AGs suit seeks permanent injunction to allow owners and independent repair providers access to repair tools (10-K, Item 7). Potential impact not estimable — if lost, could compress high-margin parts/service moat. Need to read docket and dealer economics.
  2. Precision/tech monetization: Leap Ambitions and Solutions as a Service emphasized but SaaS "did not represent significant percentage" in 2025 (10-K). Can Deere convert installed base into recurring software? What is attach rate of Operations Center subscriptions?
  3. Used equipment overhang and tariffs: Backlog PPA $4.0B at Nov 2, 2025 vs $5.2B prior year, SAT $1.9B vs $2.1B (10-K, backlog). CF backlog actually up $3.8B vs $2.2B due to planned production (10-K). $600M direct tariff impact in 2025 (10-K) — what if Supreme Court IEEPA case removes tariffs (potential recovery) or escalates? Need scenario analysis.

Universal lessons this case teaches most vividly:

  1. Cycle normalization is the valuation. Deere EPS swung from $34.63 to $18.50 in two years (an independent research service) — 46.6% drop. Capitalizing peak vs trough changes value by double. The manual's discipline — average through cycle, strip windfalls both directions — is not academic; it is the difference between thinking a $586 stock is cheap on 12x peak vs expensive on 26x normalized. Time is friend of wonderful business, enemy of mediocre, but even wonderful is cyclical.
  2. Split the conglomerate. Deere's consolidated leverage looks scary ($56.2B debt, 63% of cap, an independent research service) but equipment-only net debt $2.83B is tiny. The manual's Chapter 8 teaches: judge businesses leveraged by nature on different yardsticks. For Deere, you must do two-column mental accounting — industrial + bank — otherwise you overstate risk and understate quality.
  3. Margin of safety lives partly in quality, but discount still matters. A mediocre tractor maker at 8x trough with deteriorating ROIC would need 40-50% discount melting away. Deere at 17x normalized is fair price for quality; at 26x normalized we are paying any price for quality, which Chapter 13 never licenses. Wonderful company at fair price beats fair company at wonderful price (Manual Ch.5, 1989 letter), but wonderful at rich price is still wait.

GLOSSARY — TERMS & FORMULAS

  • Circle of competence: Businesses you can understand well enough to forecast. Formula: none. Deere: inside for long-term food demand, outside for year-to-year corn price — we normalized.
  • Owner earnings: Cash owner can take without harming competitive position. Formula: Reported earnings + D&A − Maintenance CapEx − ΔWorkingCapital. Deere 2025: $5,027M + $2,229M - $1,400M - $300M ≈ $5,556M; normalized $6,000M = $22.22/share.
  • Free Cash Flow (FCF): Operating cash flow − capital expenditures. Formula: OCF - CapEx. Deere 2025 OCF $7,459M - $1,300M ≈ $6,159M.
  • Maintenance vs growth capex: Maintenance keeps unit volume; growth expands. Split is judgment. Deere: total property spend $1.3B (10-K) vs depreciation $2,229M, so much is maintenance.
  • ROIC = NOPAT ÷ Invested Capital: Quality. NOPAT = EBIT×(1-tax). Deere equip 2025: NOPAT $3,827M =4,906×0.78, Invested $28,057M =18,887+9,170 → ROIC 13.64% =3,827÷28,057.
  • NOPAT: Net operating profit after tax = Operating Income × (1 - tax rate). Deere equip NOPAT $3,827M as above.
  • Invested Capital: Debt + Equity - Cash or net operating assets. Deere equip invested $28,057M =9,170 debt +18,887 equity.
  • ROE = Net Income ÷ Shareholders' Equity: 2025 ROE 19.4% =5,027 ÷25,950 (an independent research service and 10-K).
  • Interest Coverage = EBIT ÷ Interest Expense: Times earnings cover interest. Deere equip 13.18x =4,906÷372; consolidated ~3.7x (an independent research service).
  • Net Debt = Total Debt − Cash; Net Debt ÷ Owner Earnings: Years to repay. Consolidated $63,936M - $9,687M = $54,249M ÷ $6,000M =9.04 yrs; equipment $9,170M-$6,340M=$2,830M ÷$5,100M=0.55 yrs.
  • Current Ratio = Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities: Liquidity. Deere 2025 2.44x =83,819÷34,301.
  • Working Capital: Current Assets - Current Liabilities. Deere 2025 $49,518M =83,819-34,301.
  • Cost of Capital / Discount Rate (r): Owner's opportunity cost, gravity. Anchor: 10-yr Treasury 4.38% + risk premium 4-5% =9% base (Selection & Opinion yields).
  • Intrinsic Value = Σ OEₜ ÷ (1+r)ᵗ: Master equation, discounted future owner earnings. Base DCF $392 per share =173.19 PV explicit +219.09 discounted TV.
  • Margin of Safety = (IV − Price) ÷ IV: Cushion. Base: (390-586.86)÷390=-50.47%.
  • P/E = Price ÷ EPS: Price per $1 earnings. Deere report P/E 31.9x =567.69÷17.80 (an independent research service), median 17.0x.
  • Justified P/E: Fair multiple from growth/returns/history. Deere justified 17x = median P/E, also 1/(r-g)=1/(0.09-0.03)=16.67x.
  • EV/EBIT = Enterprise Value ÷ Operating Income; EV = Market Cap + Debt - Cash. Not primary but implied: if market cap $153B + net debt $54B = ~$207B EV, EV/EBIT 2025 EBIT ~$6,257M pretax? => ~33x, expensive.
  • Price/Book (P/B) = Price ÷ Book Value per Share. Deere book $95.98 2025 (an independent research service), P/B at $586.86 =6.11x =586.86÷95.98. ROE-justified P/B = ROE÷cost of equity =12.59%÷10%=1.26x for FS.
  • Dividend Yield = Dividend per Share ÷ Price; Payout Ratio = Dividends ÷ Net Income. Deere yield 1.1% at report (an independent research service) =6.48÷567.69; live yield 6.72÷586.86=1.15% (an independent research service 2026 est 6.72). Payout 34% 2025 =1,750÷5,027.
  • Two-stage DCF, Terminal Value, r−g spread: First explicit forecast n years, then terminal = OEₙ₊₁ ÷ (r−g). Deere terminal spread base 6.5% =9%-2.5%; TV $518.66.
  • No-growth (earning-power) value = OE ÷ r: Floor if growth stops. Deere $22.22÷0.09=$246.89.
  • Owner-earnings yield = OE ÷ Price vs long bond. Deere at base $22.22÷390=5.70% vs bond 4.38% (Selection & Opinion), superior; at price 22.22÷586.86=3.79% vs 4.38% inferior.
  • Moat / Economic franchise: Durable competitive advantage; three conditions needed/desired, no close substitute, not price regulated (Manual Ch.5). Deere moat: brand, dealer, data.
  • Economic goodwill: Franchise value above tangible capital off-balance sheet. Deere brand & network not on balance sheet, book understates.
  • One-dollar test: Retain $1 only if incremental ROIC ≥ r creates ≥$1 value. Deere: ROIC 13.6% >9% passes, so retention creates value.
  • Normalized/mid-cycle earnings: Average through cycle removing peak/trough. Deere normalized $22.22 vs peak $34.63, trough $18.50.
  • Terminal growth g: Perpetual growth after forecast, capped by economy ≤3%. Deere base 2.5%.
